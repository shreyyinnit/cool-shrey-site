About Motion Desk

Journalism that
never stands still.

Motion Desk is an independent, fully digital investigative journalism initiative. We publish evidence-based reports on matters of public interest — using open-source research, data analysis, and document review. No affiliations. No agenda. No shortcuts.

We are not a gossip page. We are not a rumour mill. Every claim links to a source. Every story survives a fact-check. If it does not hold up, it does not go out.

01

Evidence First

Every claim links to a verifiable source. No exceptions.

02

Full Transparency

Readers see exactly where our information comes from.

03

Neutral Tone

Analytical. Calm. Precise. Never emotional or activist.

04

Public Interest

Stories must matter beyond drama or spectacle.

Coverage Areas

What we
investigate.

All investigations are conducted entirely online using publicly available sources, databases, and documents.

01 / Document

Document Analysis

Government reports, policy documents, public filings, and institutional announcements reviewed for patterns and inconsistencies.

02 / Data

Data Investigations

Statistics, budget allocations, education policies, and demographic patterns that reveal what official narratives obscure.

03 / Media

Media & Misinformation

Fact-checking public claims, tracking misinformation, and analysing how narratives are constructed across media.

04 / Timeline

Timeline Investigations

Step-by-step reconstructions of events to surface inconsistencies, accountability gaps, and buried facts.

Shaurya Seth

Co-Editor-in-Chief · Founder

+

Forget the title. Founder is the least interesting thing about Shaurya, and it just happens to be the most recent thing he has built. At 19, pursuing Applied Psychology at Amity University, Lucknow, he is studying exactly what drives him: how human behaviour works, how narratives are constructed, and how language shapes the way people understand their world. He did not stumble into journalism. He architected a place for it to exist.

Motion Desk is his. From the editorial framework to the investigative workflow to the digital infrastructure, he designed and launched it from the ground up, independently. Beyond Motion Desk, he serves as the official spokesperson for Coriolis, a student-led media and writing community giving young writers and journalists a platform to publish, collaborate, and grow. He thinks in platforms, not just participation. Where others join things, Shaurya builds the thing others join.

His time as President of his school's Lexicography Club was not an extracurricular. It was an early signal. Language, for Shaurya, is not merely a tool for communication. It is an object of study in itself. He has developed a deliberate command over multiple accents, American, British, Australian, not as a party trick, but as a practised understanding of how speech shapes perception, identity, and trust. It is the kind of interest that quietly reveals how his mind works: always looking underneath the surface for the mechanism.

Model UN stages sharpened his instinct for debate and diplomatic framing. Editorial writing gave him precision. Investigative research gave him patience. The result is someone who moves between curiosity and execution with an ease that usually takes people a decade to develop.

Somewhere along the way, people started calling him Shrey. Nobody quite knows how it started. There was no moment, no announcement, no logical abbreviation. It simply happened, the way certain things do around Shaurya, quietly and all at once, and everyone just went with it. Those closest to him have not called him anything else since.

Aadya Mishra

Co-Editor-in-Chief

+

Aadya Mishra describes herself as a simple girl with a unique vision. The first part is debatable. The second part is not. At 20, pursuing Bachelor of Journalism and Mass Communication at Amity University, Lucknow, she has already co-authored an anthology, earned a black belt in Taekwondo, and built a reputation as someone who notices things others walk straight past. Perceptionist is the word she uses for herself. It fits better than most labels people spend years searching for.

Her relationship with language began early and never stayed casual. Poems, street plays, articles, research papers — Aadya does not pick a lane because she does not believe in them. She was recognised with a special award for best writing and creative expression, compiled and co-authored the anthology A Heart's News, and contributed consistently to Expressions, her college magazine, as part of the Student Editorial Board. These are not the credentials of someone who writes when inspired. They are the credentials of someone who writes because stopping is not an option.

In school, she was House Captain and a member of the Student Council — roles that signal less about authority and more about the kind of person others instinctively orient toward. On the debate stage, she learned that a strong argument is only as good as the attention you paid before you opened your mouth. That lesson shows up in everything she does. Aadya listens first. It is why her writing lands the way it does, and why her reporting will too.

The Taekwondo medals, earned at every level from local to national, are not a footnote. They are a character reference. Discipline, patience, the willingness to take a hit and recalibrate — these are not athletic qualities. They are editorial ones. Motion Desk runs on exactly that kind of temperament.

She says you can expect anything and everything from her. That is not bravado. It is just an honest description of someone whose range has never been her limitation.
